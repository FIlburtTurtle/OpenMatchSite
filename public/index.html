About

Urban Developers, Inc., is a developer and manager of retail and residential mixed-use projects in highly populated urban areas with a focus on transit-oriented sites.

Mission

To conceive, design and execute transit-oriented, sustainable, mixed-use village nodes in large urban settings where people can live, shop and play with security in a walkable environment.

Purpose

To acquire and build quality projects that serve their communities, are sustainable, well-located, and economically attractive to institutional and high net worth investors.

Focus

To introduce new developments or adaptive use of existing assets to urban locations in metro areas of San Francisco, Los Angeles, and San Diego, where they will be transformative to their neighborhoods.

Properties

The metropolitan areas of Los Angeles, San Francisco and San Diego are uniquely positioned to transform how we have chosen to live over the past 60 years. There is a growing demand for livable urban communities close to transit nodes with convenient, walkable access to amenities and services.

San Francisco

We currently have 314 apartment units and 250,000 SF of retail shops space under development in two mixed use transit oriented developments scheduled to start construction in 2015 and 2016.

San Diego

The San Diego metropolitan trade area has great opportunity to create transit oriented urban developments. We are currently evaluating various development opportunities in the San Diego metropolitan area.

Los Angeles

Alan Dibartolomeo recently completed the Eleve Lofts, 208 apartment units with 26,000 square feet of ground floor retail space located across from Pasadena’s Americana Life Style Center. Eleve features roof deck amenities including fire pits, hot tubs, a dog park and a children’s play area. We are looking for more mixed use development opportunities in the greater Los Angeles trade area.

What We Do

As experts in commercial as well as multi-family residential we address and respect the divergent needs of retailers and residents.

As experts in commercial as well as multi-family residential we address and respect the divergent needs of retailers and residents. The key to executing well anchored mixed-use development is delivering a finished product that is exciting for all stakeholders, from the retail anchor stores to the residents. We feel that the creation of successful mixed-use developments is part art and part science, where the balancing of demands to create a truly acceptable site is the result of decades of experience.

Our relationships with credit retail tenants allows us to start the process of design based on the demands and requirements of an anchor tenant use. We add to that the experience with vertical multi-family development on small urban sites to solve the complexities of access, security and exceptional views for the residential component.

As municipalities trend toward requiring mixed-use developments to densify their downtowns, we have focused our growth toward retailers who control sites and want an experienced developer to add the residential component in an economic and sensitive design on the site.

Team

Our 30 years of experience allows us to respect and address the divergent needs of retailers and residents on the same site.

ALAN D. DIBARTOLOMEO

Alan Dibartolomeo, president of A&D Investment Group, Inc., brings 30 years of acquisition, development and management experience to the institutional grade real estate entities he directs. In the 2000’s, Mr. Dibartolomeo served in-house as Development/Construction advisor for Legacy Partners Residential Irvine, responsible for a seasoned team of professionals overseeing project completion of four apartment communities totaling over 900 units. In the business cycles encompassing the 1980s and 1990s, Mr. Dibartolomeo acquired, built teams and managed over a million square feet in Southern California in posi- tions with two of the most highly regarded commercial developers in the country, Trammell Crow Company and Spieker Properties. He has a wide range of experience encompassing housing, oce, industrial and retail entitlement and development.

Mr. Dibartolomeo has been a guest lecturer at the USC Masters in Real Estate Development graduate program, the USC Ross Minority Program in Real Estate, and Urban Land Institute presentations. Mr. Dibar- tolomeo has an undergraduate degree from UCLA and an MBA from Harvard University. He is married with three children, and resides in Orange County, California.

BILL MITCHELL

Mr. Mitchell’s professional career started in the general contracting business in the mid 1980’s building office buildings, shopping centers and apartment projects. He earned a Bachelor of Science Degree from Cal Poly University while working as a general contractor. Upon graduation he entered the real estate development business. Within five years he became a partner in a real estate development company developing properties throughout five western states. While a partner of Riley Pearlman Mitchell Company he developed 26 projects throughout California and northern Nevada. Some of the tenants in these projects included Target Stores, JC Penney, Walgreens, Safeway Inc., Raley’s, Wal-Mart, Home Depot, Costco, Chase Bank, Wells Fargo Bank, Union Bank, Starbucks Coffee and many other national firms.

From 1996 to 2013 as president of WT Mitchel Group Inc. he completed 17 ground-up retail and mixed-use projects. He has personally supervised the land acquisition, governmental approvals, leasing, construction and management of commercial projects ranging in size from a $200 thousand dollar T.I. to a $100 million dollar mixed-use development. He has successfully negotiated leases, purchase contracts and development agreements with many of the country’s most sophisticated companies.

In 2013, responding to a growing need for well-designed and integrated urban mixed-use developments, Mr. Mitchell formed Urban Developers Inc., with Mr. Dibartolomeo. Urban Developers currently has two transit-oriented mixed- use developments totaling 324 apartment homes and 260,000 square feet of commercial space in development.
